# Working at a Hyper Growth Startup

![office](https://source.unsplash.com/BC_lzmF2R94/1600x900)

*Photo by [Vitaly Gariev](https://unsplash.com/@silverkblack) on [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com)*

100 hours per week don’t feel like it if you’re in it. You’re just busy: talking to colleagues, friends, traveling, and constantly context-switching.

It also doesn’t feel like it when the work is meaningful and top management requests you. You don’t second-guess; you just execute.

But you still need to stay sane and take breaks to think. For me, running in the mountains with music and being alone really helps. It gives me space to process what is going on: what to do, what not to do, and how to add value and follow up with people.

It also makes me feel like I did something for my body, and it allows my mind to work better afterwards. It’s my daily mini requirement for calling a day successful beyond work.

Some tradeoffs are unavoidable.

- Giving up: I have to give up playing pickleball. I can’t keep up with friends who have more time, and it doesn’t feel as efficient to me as a workout.
- I have to be selective with who I talk to, and what I agree to do outside of work, to protect my time.

I also re-read the book *This Naked Mind*, which helped me stop drinking again, and I focused on healthier food so that I don’t feel bad.

Many meetings with colleagues became friendships. We do hackathons, but we also talk to top management — and everybody is really kind and understanding. I love the blameless culture and how professional everybody is.

And I also see gaps and work on actively filling them.
