# Card Games on the Go With One Deck

A single 54-card deck is one of the best travel tools ever invented.

It fits in a jacket pocket, works in airports, cabins, hostels, and campsites, and can support very different moods: tactical, chaotic, social, quiet, loud, fast, and surprisingly competitive.

These are some of my favorite games to play on the road with one standard deck. Some use the jokers, some ignore them, and one works best after trimming the deck down.

## Quick Comparison

| Game | Players | Deck | Best For | Core Hook |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Yaniv | 2-8 | Full 54-card deck | Low-score tension | Drop your hand low enough to call safely |
| Palace / Shithead | 2-6 | Full deck | Casual chaos | Shed cards and survive special ranks |
| Crazy Eights | 2-6 | Full deck | Mixed-skill groups | Match suit or rank, use wild eights |
| Gin Rummy | 2 | Standard deck, jokers removed | Focused two-player play | Build melds and manage deadwood |
| Rummy 500 | 2-6 | Standard deck, jokers usually removed | Swingy open rummy | The discard pile becomes a resource |
| Egyptian Rat Screw | 2-6 | Full deck | High-energy groups | Fast flips and slapping patterns |
| Durak | 2-6 | 36-card deck | Attack-defense play | Beat attacks and avoid being last |
| President | 4-7 | Full deck | Social climbing | Play higher sets and escape first |

## 1. Yaniv

Yaniv, sometimes spelled Yannif, is a popular Israeli rummy-style game. The goal is simple: have the lowest hand total when someone calls "Yaniv!"

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 8, best with 3 to 5 |
| Deck | 54 cards including 2 jokers |
| Goal | Have the lowest total when a player calls Yaniv |
| Main tension | Calling early enough to win, but not so early that someone beats you with an Asaf |

### Card Values

| Card | Value |
|---|---:|
| Ace | 1 |
| 2 through 10 | Face value |
| Jack, Queen, King | 10 |
| Joker | 0 |

Some tables play a Joker as the value of the card it replaces, but `0` is the standard rule.

### Setup

1. Deal 5 cards face down to each player.
2. Turn 1 card face up to start the discard pile.
3. Place the rest of the deck face down as the draw pile.

### Legal Discards

| Discard Type | Requirement | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Single card | Any one card | `9 of clubs` |
| Set | 2, 3, or 4 cards of the same rank | `7 of hearts + 7 of spades + 7 of clubs` |
| Straight | 3 or more cards in sequence of the same suit | `4-5-6 of hearts` |

A Joker can be used in the middle of a straight.

### How a Turn Works

| Step | What Happens | Important Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Discard | Play a single card, set, or straight | You must discard something |
| Slap / Cut | Other players may instantly throw matching cards onto a multi-card discard | First valid slap counts; a wrong slap usually earns a penalty card |
| Draw | Take 1 card from the draw pile or from your own discard | If you discarded a set or straight, you may only take the first or last card, not a middle card |

### Calling Yaniv

| Situation | Result |
|---|---|
| You have `7` or less on your turn | You may call "Yaniv!" instead of taking a normal turn |
| Your hand is the lowest | You win the round and everyone else scores their hand value |
| Another player is equal or lower | You get an **Asaf**: `30 penalty points + your hand total`, and the actual lowest hand scores `0` |

Some tables use `5` instead of `7` as the threshold.

### Scoring

| Rule | Effect |
|---|---|
| Standard loss condition | First player to go over `100` or `200` points loses, depending on table rules |
| Winner of the full game | Lowest total score |
| Exact `100` or `200` | Some groups cut the score in half to `50` or `100` |
| Exact `50`, `100`, `150`, or `200` | Some groups award a `-50` bonus |

Yaniv is excellent because it combines hand management, reaction speed, and nerve.

## 2. Palace / Shithead

This is one of the best casual travel games because the objective is intuitive: get rid of all your cards before everyone else.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 6 |
| Deck | Standard deck, jokers usually removed |
| Goal | Be the first player to get rid of all cards |
| Main tension | Deciding when to burn, reset, or risk picking up the pile |

### Setup

1. Give each player 3 face-down cards.
2. Place 3 face-up cards on top of those.
3. Give each player 3 cards in hand.
4. Leave the rest as a draw pile and start a central play pile.

### How It Works

| Phase | Rule |
|---|---|
| Normal play | Play a card equal to or higher than the current top card |
| Multiples | You can usually play matching ranks together |
| Refill | While the draw pile still exists, players commonly draw back up to 3 cards |
| If stuck | Pick up the whole pile |
| Endgame | Once your hand is gone, play face-up cards, then face-down cards blindly |

### Common Special Cards

| Card | Typical Effect |
|---|---|
| `2` | Reset or wild card |
| `10` | Burns and clears the pile |

House rules vary a lot, which is part of the charm.

## 3. Crazy Eights

Crazy Eights is fast, simple, and especially good with mixed-skill groups or tired travelers.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 6 |
| Deck | Standard deck, jokers removed |
| Goal | Be the first player to empty your hand |
| Main tension | Keeping flexible suits and timing your wild eights |

### Setup

| Players | Cards Dealt |
|---|---:|
| 2 players | 7 cards each |
| 3 to 6 players | 5 cards each |

Turn one card face up to start the discard pile.

### Turn Rules

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| Normal play | Match either the rank or suit of the top discard |
| Wild card | An `8` is wild and lets you name the next suit |
| If you cannot play | Some groups draw one card, others draw until a playable card appears |
| Winner | First player with no cards left |

## 4. Gin Rummy

Gin Rummy is cleaner and more tactical than Yaniv. It is one of the best two-player games you can carry anywhere.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 |
| Deck | Standard deck, jokers removed |
| Goal | Build melds and minimize deadwood |
| Main tension | Taking useful cards without giving away too much information |

### Setup

1. Deal 10 cards to each player.
2. Turn one card face up for the discard pile.
3. Leave the rest as the stock.

### Core Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Set | Cards of the same rank |
| Run | Cards in sequence of the same suit |
| Deadwood | Cards not included in melds |
| Knock | End the round when your deadwood is low enough, commonly `10` or less |
| Gin | All cards are fully melded |

### Turn Structure

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Draw from the stock or the top of the discard pile |
| 2 | Improve your hand by building toward sets and runs |
| 3 | Discard one card |

Gin Rummy rewards memory, restraint, and careful discard choices.

## 5. Rummy 500

Rummy 500 feels more open and swingy than Gin because the discard pile becomes a real strategic resource.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 6 |
| Deck | Standard deck, jokers usually removed |
| Goal | Be the first player to reach `500` points |
| Main tension | Whether to take a deep discard and reveal your plan |

### Setup

1. Deal 7 cards to each player for a casual version.
2. Turn one card face up for the discard pile.
3. Leave the rest as the stock.

### Turn Structure

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Draw from the stock or take a card from the discard pile |
| 2 | If you take from the discard pile, you usually must immediately use that target card in a meld |
| 3 | Lay down new melds or add to existing ones |
| 4 | Discard one card |

### Scoring Idea

| Rule | Effect |
|---|---|
| Melded cards | Score their value toward your total |
| Game end | First player to `500` points wins |

## 6. Egyptian Rat Screw

This is the loudest game on the list. It is reflex-based, slap-heavy, and funny when everyone is awake enough to compete.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 6 |
| Deck | Full deck |
| Goal | Win all the cards |
| Main tension | Spot patterns and slap faster than everyone else |

### Setup

Deal the deck as evenly as possible. Each player keeps a face-down personal stack.

### Core Turn Rules

| Situation | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Normal turn | Flip one card face up into the center |
| Face card challenge | The next player gets limited chances to answer with another face card |
| Failed challenge | The player who started the challenge wins the pile |

### Face Card Chances

| Card Played | Response Chances |
|---|---:|
| Jack | 1 |
| Queen | 2 |
| King | 3 |
| Ace | 4 |

### Common Slap Rules

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Doubles | Two identical ranks in a row |
| Sandwich | Same rank with one card between them |

First correct slap takes the pile. Wrong slaps usually cost you cards.

## 7. Durak

Durak is one of the best attack-and-defense card games around. The rules are short, but the decisions get sharp quickly.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 2 to 6 |
| Deck | 36-card deck |
| Goal | Get rid of all your cards and avoid being last |
| Main tension | Press attacks without overcommitting trump cards |

### Deck Adjustment

With a standard 54-card deck, remove everything below `6` and ignore the jokers.

### Setup

1. Deal 6 cards to each player.
2. Reveal one card to set the trump suit.
3. Leave the rest as the draw pile.

### Battle Rules

| Role | Rule |
|---|---|
| Attacker | Plays a card to start the attack |
| Defender | Must beat it with a higher card of the same suit or any trump |
| Follow-up attack | Other attackers may add cards matching ranks already on the table |
| Failed defense | Defender picks up everything |
| Successful defense | All cards are discarded |
| Refill | Players draw back up to 6 cards after the battle |

The last player still holding cards is the **Durak**, or fool.

## 8. President / Asshole / Scum

This is a very social climbing game where high cards rule and table politics start almost immediately.

### Quick Facts

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Players | 4 to 7 |
| Deck | Full deck; jokers depend on the table |
| Goal | Empty your hand before everyone else |
| Main tension | Deciding when to spend high cards and when to pass |

### Setup

Deal the entire deck as evenly as possible.

### Round Flow

| Phase | Rule |
|---|---|
| Lead | Start with a single card, pair, or matching set depending on house rules |
| Follow | Each next player must play the same number of cards in a higher rank, or pass |
| Clear | When everyone passes, the pile clears |
| New lead | The last successful player starts the next round |
| Result | First player out wins the round; last player out loses it |

Many groups add rank-based privileges in later rounds, which is where the President and Scum names come from.

## Which One to Pick

| Situation | Best Pick |
|---|---|
| I want low-score rummy tension | Yaniv |
| I want casual chaos | Palace |
| I need something teachable in 2 minutes | Crazy Eights |
| We are exactly two people and want a sharper game | Gin Rummy |
| We want open-ended rummy with more drama | Rummy 500 |
| The group is loud and wants reflexes | Egyptian Rat Screw |
| I want attack-defense strategy | Durak |
| We have a bigger group and want social competition | President |

One deck really is enough for a whole trip.
